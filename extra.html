Pokémon: MY First Mewvie - Mew Strikes Back

intro synopsis critics rebuttal

so, I was in a movie...

Pokémon: The First Movie - Mewtwo Strikes Back (1998), also referred to as simply Pokémon: The First Movie, is—as implied—the first movie released by the Pokémon™ franchise. It is an anime fantasy adventure film, directed by the one and only Kunihiko Yuyama, who has also directed most of the Pokémon shows and movies. It was originally released in Japan on July 18, 1998, with the extended version airing on Japanese television nearly a year later on July 8, 1999. Later that year, the English-adaption of the film was released for North American audiences by Warner Bros. Pictures on November 10. Not only was this the first Pokémon™ franchise film, it was also my first movie, or mewvie if you will. This was my big break as an actor! I was not exactly acting though. It was more so a documentary that revealed the schemes of Team Rocket to create a living weapon using my DNA. Personally, I am flattered that they think so highly of me, enough that they wanted to clone me.

Also, you may be wondering, Mew, are you bilingual? Why yes, I am! 私は日本語と英語の両方を流暢に話します。That means I am fluent in both Japanese and English in Japanese. However, I do not have many speaking roles in much of the media from the Pokémon™ franchise, because they think I am dangerously hilarious and I will end the careers of every comedy to ever exist. So, when the cameras are rolling, I and all the other Pokémon keep quiet and stick to just saying our names.

movie synopsis

The events in this film occurred over a quarter of a century ago, so I will try my best to accurately recall them. Pokémon: The First Movie involves both Mew and Mewtwo. The movie depicts the origins of Mewtwo, who was genetically made from my DNA. He is a copycat—quite literally—and wants to be me so bad! Giovianni, the leader of the mischievous of the crime syndicate Team Rocket, hired Dr. Fuji to create a living weapon using one of my eyelashes. Giovianni specifically sought out Dr. Fuji for this because he gained expertise in cloning after his daughter Amber died and he dedicated himself to trying to bring her back.

addressing the haters

According to Rotten Tomatoes, the audience score from over 50,000 users on average rated Pokémon: The First Movie at 73%. Many of these reviews consist of nostalgic adults that recently rewatched a movie from their childhood. The Tomatometer® rating represents the opinions of professional critics. In general the ratings of the Tomatometer® rating is deemed more demonstrative of a film's cinematic quality, while the audience rating is a reflection how enjoyable a movie (for the target audience typically). According to Premium Beat, audience scores may have lost some of their merit.

my rebuttal: MEW strikes back!!!

Despite these criticisms online, the movie is not horrible as the Tomatometer® make it out to be. In fact, out of the 78 Pokémon movies listed on Rotten Tomatoes (most of which have no Tomatometer score), Pokémon: The First Movie isn't even the lowest ranked one. Tied with a Tomatometer score of 16% is Pokémon 4Ever (2002), which critics say is only for die hard fans. The audience score is lower for Pokémon 4Ever at only 54%, which obviously means that my movie is better. Coming in last place with a whopping 0% rating from film critics is Pokémon: Mewtwo Returns (2000). However, there are only five Tomatometer reviews for this one as compared to 91 and 38 for Pokémon: The First Movie and Pokémon 4Ever respectively. Pokémon: Mewtwo Returns has a higher audience score at 60% as compared to Pokémon 4Ever, which both have a similar number of reviews. Also, to reiterate, Pokémon: The First Movie easily clears both of these movies in terms of audience preference.

Now, that we've establised that Pokémon: The First Movie is NOT the worst Pokémon movie, we can bring to explore what makes it such an excellent film.
